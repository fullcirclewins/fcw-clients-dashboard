⚡ FCW Impact · Week 1
$86,774 · 14,754 Sessions in First Managed Week
FCW's first full week produced $86,774 in gross sales — 3.6× the baseline average. Sessions surged to 14,754 (+145%) with stroller-specific sessions at 11,337, and Platform CVR hit 2.67% vs. a 0.64% baseline average. The combination of Stroller 3.0 and the full accessory catalog drove the strongest week in the dataset.
🎯 Brand vs Category Search
Brand Search Held Steady at 5,833
Brand Search held at 5,833 in the FCW week, consistent with the 4,162–5,947 range across all 10 weeks. Category Search dropped to 2,874 in the FCW week — shifts in product launch and ad strategy may have contributed to this change. As the brand and Stroller 3.0 establish organic rank, we expect to see a natural shift in category search behavior over time.
📦 Accessories: Bundle Behavior
92.5% of Orders Were Single-Item · 7.5% Multi-Item
Of 748 orders from 2/24–3/11, 56 (7.5%) included 2+ items: 46 paired a stroller with at least one accessory, and 10 were orders with 2 or more accessories without a stroller. The goal is stroller + accessory adoption — monitoring these bundle patterns will help guide virtual bundle strategy. Recommend tracking D2C accessory revenue alongside Amazon to understand the full picture as accessories scale.
📉 The 2/21 Pre-Launch Trough
15 Purchases · $8,625 — The Natural Low Point
The week ending Feb 21 was the lowest point — only 15 purchases and $8,625 in sales. This reflects a natural transition as Stroller 2.0 listings wound down ahead of the Feb 24 launch. The prior agency's CAC hit $143 this week. The subsequent recovery to 63 purchases (2/28) and then 282 (3/7) makes the trajectory all the more striking.
💰 AOV Shift: What It Means
Lower AOV · Volume More Than Compensates
AOV fell from $575 (stroller-only baseline) to $342 at launch and $220 in the FCW week as accessories entered the unit mix. Volume more than compensates — gross revenue tripled. The goal will be to find ways to increase bundling, such as leveraging virtual bundles to help increase AOV and sell a complete collection. Shifts in content along with the brand store launch should help support scale in this goal. Stroller revenue alone was $58,194 in the FCW week — still +2.4× the baseline.
📈 Saved for Later → Retargeting Pipeline
182 Saved · Retargeting Launched on AMS 3/10
Saved for Later jumped from 58 (2/28) to 182 (3/7) — +214%. These are high-intent shoppers who've engaged deeply but haven't converted. FCW launched retargeting campaigns on AMS on 3/10 with low spend specifically to capture this latent demand pool.
Key Takeaway: Generic/category search remains the primary channel for capturing market share and new-to-brand customers. The critical story in March is efficiency: Generic ROAS improved from 0.35× in February to 2.44× in March — a 600% gain. By tightening efficiency across both brand and generic spend, FCW is doing more with the same budget, enabling greater investment in category awareness to take competitive market share rather than spending to capture customers already searching for Mockingbird.

FCW's approach: Generic and category search terms are the primary channel for capturing competitive market share and reaching new-to-brand customers. By tightening efficiency across both brand and generic spend — improving match types, negative keywords, and bid strategy — FCW is generating more output from the same budget. This creates the capacity to invest more aggressively in category awareness and competitive conquesting, taking market share rather than concentrating spend on customers already searching specifically for Mockingbird. The pace of optimization reflects this commitment: Amazon history shows 164 ad changes across all of January and February combined — in the first 10 days of March alone, FCW made 119 changes. That density of iteration directly drove the efficiency gains and accelerated generic adoption visible in the data.
